## Design Context

### Users
SDAL Sosyal serves three distinct groups within the same high school community: **current students**, **teachers**, and **alumni**. Ages span from teenagers to longtime graduates, so designs must be legible and inviting across a wide range of tech literacy. The primary job is reconnection and belonging — checking in on peers, sharing moments, keeping up with school life. Users open the app the way they'd walk into a familiar common room, not a productivity dashboard.

### Brand Personality
Trustworthy, warm, modern. The product should feel like coming home — familiar, unhurried, socially alive. It celebrates school identity and community continuity without becoming gimmicky or noisy. It is contemporary enough for students but dignified enough for teachers and alumni who have been away for years.

### Aesthetic Direction
Built on an earthy parchment-and-cream palette (`--bg: #f3efe8`, accent `#ff6b4a`/`#C8633C`) with 'Manrope' for body and 'Space Grotesk' for the brand mark. 16–24px radius everywhere. Layered warm surfaces, soft borders, and generous whitespace convey approachability. Motion is sophisticated (5 distinct View Transition families already in place) and should always feel like natural movement through a familiar space — never jarring or performative. Both light and dark modes are fully supported. The app is bilingual (Turkish + English); designs must hold up in both.

### Design Principles
1. **Home, not homepage** — every screen should evoke the feeling of arriving somewhere familiar. Favor warmth, social presence, and recognizable faces over abstract layouts.
2. **Generational legibility** — typography and information hierarchy must be clear for a 16-year-old and a 50-year-old teacher equally. When in doubt, go larger and clearer.
3. **Warm restraint** — the palette is earthy, not flashy. Avoid cold blues, aggressive contrast, corporate greys, and neon. Let the accent earn attention sparingly.
4. **Motion as welcome** — transitions and micro-interactions should feel like natural room-to-room movement, not UI spectacle. Already established transition families should be the model.
5. **Trust through consistency** — always use design tokens (`--accent`, `--card`, `--ink`, etc.); never hardcode values. Consistency is the silent promise that this is a place they can rely on.
