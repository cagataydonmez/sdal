## Design Context

### Users
SDAL serves alumni who come to the platform to stay socially connected with one another while also keeping a line of sight to the broader community and professional network. On the feed page, the primary job is helping members quickly feel the pulse of the alumni network through posts, stories, messages, online presence, and timely updates.

### Brand Personality
Warm, proud, contemporary. The product should feel welcoming and socially alive, with a clear sense of school identity and community continuity. It should celebrate participation and presence without becoming gimmicky or noisy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the existing warm editorial palette as the base and lean into noticeable personality through layered surfaces, expressive composition, and refined motion. Favor subtle celebration, social energy, and moments of discovery over playful novelty. The feed should feel current and vibrant, not corporate-flat and not overly whimsical.

### Design Principles
1. Make community presence visible: show signs of activity, participation, and shared momentum wherever possible.
2. Use delight as emphasis, not interruption: motion and decorative details should support scanning, posting, and replying.
3. Preserve warmth with structure: pair soft materials and inviting color with crisp hierarchy and contemporary rhythm.
4. Reward interaction: hover, focus, and refresh states should feel alive and responsive, especially on repeat-use surfaces.
5. Keep the feed socially legible: important actions and signals should remain fast to parse on both desktop and mobile.
