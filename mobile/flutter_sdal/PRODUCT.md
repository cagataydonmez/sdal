# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SDAL Sosyal serves the full SDAL community: alumni, current students where permitted, teachers, administrators, and other verified community members. They use the Flutter app on mobile to stay connected, follow school and alumni activity, message members, discover people, join groups, track events and announcements, share photos, and respond to opportunities without feeling like they have entered a generic school system or a public social network.

## Product Purpose

The Flutter app is the mobile home for SDAL's private community. It brings the existing SDAL backend into a focused, trusted, everyday mobile experience for community news, member discovery, messaging, notifications, profile management, albums, events, jobs, and admin workflows. Success means members can quickly understand what is new, find relevant people or opportunities, act with confidence, and feel that the product belongs specifically to SDAL rather than to a generic portal template.

## Brand Personality

Warm, trusted, lively. The app should feel welcoming and human, but still credible enough for identity, verification, consent, messaging, and administrative actions. It should support a community rhythm: active, social, and current, without chasing the performative habits of public social media.

## Anti-references

Do not make the app feel like a generic school portal, a corporate LinkedIn clone, a childish social app, a heavy admin panel, a Facebook-like feed, or an Instagram-like media app. Avoid generic education software cues, overbuilt dashboards, vanity engagement patterns, trend-driven social-media chrome, and interface decisions that make SDAL feel interchangeable with any other institution.

## Design Principles

1. Community before platform: design interactions around SDAL relationships, shared history, and useful member context rather than generic social mechanics.
2. Trust is visible: identity, verification, consent, privacy-sensitive actions, and admin states should be clear, calm, and easy to recover from.
3. Mobile momentum matters: common jobs should be reachable quickly, with lightweight flows that respect short sessions and one-handed use.
4. Liveliness with restraint: the app can feel warm and active through rhythm, color, motion, and timely content, but it should never become noisy or childish.
5. Local specificity wins: copy, structure, and visual details should feel native to SDAL's Turkish-first community while preserving English support.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline for contrast, touch targets, focus order, semantic labels, and readable text. Support reduced-motion preferences for nonessential animation. Do not rely on color alone for status, verification, errors, or admin states; pair color with icons, labels, or structure. Preserve Turkish and English localization quality, and design for members with different technical confidence levels.
